Save The Bees!


From a study done by BeeInformed.org, nearly 44% of U.S. bee colonies were lost from 2019 to 2020. Losses during Summer 2019 marked the highest ever recorded by BIP during that season, whereas losses during Winter 2019/2020 were the second lowest on record.


Data Source: BeeInformed.org


Why Bees are Important

Each year, bees are dying off in massive numbers throughout the U.S. and Canada. Bees polinate one-third of the food we eat today including fruits, vegetables, seeds, nuts and more foods important to our diet. However, bees are in danger. More than half of the 4,000 native bee species to North America are declining in number, and 1 in 4 species are at risk of extinction.-TheBeeConservacy

  • How are Bees at risk?

    There are many different ways Bees are at risk. Some of the primary ones are Climate Change, Habitat Loss, Chemival Pesticides, Invasive Plant Species and Diseases/Parasites.

    What is the impact of losing the Bees?

    Bees are know for their production of beeswax and honey, and also for their extensive role in argiculture through pollination of fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds and more.

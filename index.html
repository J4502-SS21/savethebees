Why Bees are Important

Each year, bees are experiencing massive die-offs throughout the U.S. and Canada. Bees polinate one-third of the food we eat today including fruits, vegetables, seeds, nuts and more all important to our diet. However, bees are in danger. More than half of the 4,000 native bee species to North America are declining in number, and 1 in 4 species are at risk of extinction.

How are Bees at risk?

There are many different threats to pollinators. Some of the primary ones are Habitat Loss, Climate Change, Chemival Pesticides, Invasive Plant Species and Diseases/Parasites.

What is the impact of losing the Bees?

Bees are know for their production of beeswax and honey, and also for their extensive role in argiculture through pollination of fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds and more.
